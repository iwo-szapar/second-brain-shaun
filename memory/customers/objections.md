# Common Objections & Responses

> How to address concerns and move deals forward.

## Overview

Objections are buying signals in disguise. This document captures common objections and proven responses. Use `/recall objection [topic]` to find relevant responses.

## Objection Categories

### Price Objections

#### "It's too expensive"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Haven't seen enough value yet
- Budget constraints are real
- Comparing to wrong alternatives

**Response Framework**:
1. Acknowledge: "I understand budget is a consideration..."
2. Explore: "Help me understand what you're comparing this to..."
3. Reframe: "Let's look at the cost of [not solving the problem]..."
4. Prove: "[Specific ROI data or customer example]"

**Proof Points**:
- [Customer X saved $Y in Z months]
- [ROI calculation or case study]

**Scripts**:
> "I hear you. Let me ask - what's the current cost of [pain point]? Most of our customers find that [product] pays for itself within [timeframe] because [reason]."

---

#### "I can get something cheaper"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Focused on price, not value
- May not understand differentiation
- Looking for permission to go cheap

**Response Framework**:
1. Agree: "You absolutely can find cheaper options..."
2. Differentiate: "Here's what you'd be giving up..."
3. Risk: "Our customers who tried cheaper first found..."
4. Value: "The real question is what [outcome] is worth to you..."

**Proof Points**:
- [Why cheaper alternatives fail]
- [Switch stories from cheap competitors]

---

#### "We don't have budget"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Not a priority yet
- Need to find/reallocate budget
- Need help building the case

**Response Framework**:
1. Validate: "Budget is always a consideration..."
2. Explore: "Is this a timing issue or a priority issue?"
3. Help: "Would it help if I provided [ROI calculator/business case]?"
4. Alternative: "Some customers start with [smaller package]..."

**Next Steps**:
- Offer to help build internal business case
- Identify budget cycles/timing
- Explore starter options

### Timing Objections

#### "Not the right time"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Other priorities right now
- Change is hard
- Not urgent enough yet

**Response Framework**:
1. Understand: "What's driving your timeline?"
2. Urgency: "What's the cost of waiting [X months]?"
3. Easy start: "Most customers are surprised how quickly they see results..."
4. Future: "When would be better timing? Let me..."

**Scripts**:
> "I get it - timing matters. Can I ask what would need to change for this to become a priority? And what's happening in [timeframe] that makes it better timing?"

---

#### "We're in the middle of [other initiative]"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Resources are stretched
- Don't want more change
- Need to focus

**Response Framework**:
1. Respect: "That makes sense - [initiative] is important..."
2. Connect: "Actually, [product] can help with [initiative] by..."
3. Lightweight: "Many customers run this alongside [initiative] because..."
4. Timing: "When does [initiative] wrap up?"

---

### Trust Objections

#### "We've been burned before"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Previous bad experience
- Risk averse now
- Need extra proof

**Response Framework**:
1. Empathize: "I'm sorry to hear that. What happened?"
2. Differentiate: "Here's how we're different..."
3. Reduce risk: "That's why we offer [guarantee/pilot/references]..."
4. Proof: "Would it help to speak with [similar customer]?"

**Risk Reducers**:
- [Guarantee or pilot program]
- [Reference customers]
- [Case studies in their industry]

---

#### "How do I know this will work for us?"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Need social proof
- Worried about implementation
- Want validation

**Response Framework**:
1. Validate: "That's a smart question..."
2. Similar: "We've worked with [X similar companies]..."
3. Specifics: "For example, [relevant case study]..."
4. Offer: "Would you like to speak with [customer like them]?"

---

### Competitor Objections

#### "We're looking at [Competitor]"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Doing due diligence
- May prefer competitor
- Want to understand differences

**Response Framework**:
1. Positive: "[Competitor] is a [genuine compliment]..."
2. Differentiate: "The key difference is [our unique value]..."
3. Fit: "Companies typically choose us when [specific need]..."
4. Open: "What's most important to you in making this decision?"

**Competitor Comparisons**:
See: `memory/company/differentiators.md`

---

#### "We already have a solution"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Status quo is comfortable
- Need strong reason to switch
- Change requires effort

**Response Framework**:
1. Acknowledge: "How's that working for you?"
2. Probe: "What would make you consider a change?"
3. Gap: "Most customers who switch to us were frustrated by [gap]..."
4. Easy: "We make switching easy with [migration support]..."

---

### Authority Objections

#### "I need to run this by [person]"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Not the decision maker
- Need buy-in from others
- Want support for their recommendation

**Response Framework**:
1. Support: "Of course - what would [person] need to see?"
2. Prepare: "Let me give you [materials] to share..."
3. Offer: "Would it help if I joined that conversation?"
4. Coach: "What do you think [person]'s main concerns would be?"

---

#### "Our team won't adopt this"

**What They Really Mean**:
- Change management concern
- Past adoption failures
- Need training/support

**Response Framework**:
1. Common: "Adoption is always a consideration..."
2. Ease: "Here's how we make adoption easy..."
3. Proof: "[Company] had similar concerns and achieved [adoption rate]..."
4. Support: "We include [training/onboarding/support]..."

## Objection Prevention

### In Discovery
- Surface objections early: "What concerns do you have?"
- Pre-handle: "Some customers initially worry about [X], here's why..."

### In Proposal
- Address top objections proactively
- Include ROI/proof in materials
- Offer risk reducers upfront

### In Negotiation
- Don't discount first - add value
- Trade concessions for commitments
- Know your walk-away points
