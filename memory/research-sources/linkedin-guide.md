# LinkedIn Research Guide

## Purpose
Extract maximum value from LinkedIn for job application research.

**Primary Sources**:
1. Company LinkedIn page
2. Hiring manager LinkedIn profile
3. Employee profiles
4. Company posts and updates

---

## Company Page Research

### What to Look For

**Company Overview Section**
- [ ] Industry and specialties
- [ ] Company size (employee count)
- [ ] Headquarters location
- [ ] Founded date
- [ ] Company tagline/description

**About Section**
- [ ] Mission statement
- [ ] Products/services description
- [ ] Key value propositions
- [ ] Website link

**Recent Posts & Activity**
- [ ] Last 5-10 posts
- [ ] Engagement level (likes, comments)
- [ ] Content themes
- [ ] Product launches or news
- [ ] Culture signals

**People Section**
- [ ] Leadership team
- [ ] Key department heads
- [ ] Total employee count
- [ ] Employee growth trends
- [ ] Shared connections

**Jobs Section**
- [ ] Other open positions
- [ ] Hiring patterns
- [ ] Team growth areas

---

## Research Workflow

### Step 1: Company Page (10 min)

1. **Quick Scan** (2 min)
   - Company size and industry
   - Recent activity (last 30 days)
   - Tagline and description

2. **Deep Dive** (5 min)
   - Read last 5-10 posts
   - Note any product launches or news
   - Check "About" for mission/values
   - Review "People" for team structure

3. **Extract Insights** (3 min)
   - What makes them unique?
   - Recent achievements or milestones?
   - Culture signals from posts?
   - Growth indicators?

### Step 2: Job Posting (5 min)

1. **Job Details**
   - Copy exact job title
   - Note key responsibilities (top 3-5)
   - Required vs. nice-to-have skills
   - Team/department info

2. **Context Clues**
   - Why is role open? (expansion, new function)
   - Success metrics mentioned?
   - Tools/tech mentioned?
   - Reporting structure?

3. **Language Analysis**
   - Key phrases used
   - Tone (formal, casual, technical)
   - Values emphasized
   - Red flags or concerns?

### Step 3: Hiring Manager (5-10 min if available)

1. **Profile Basics**
   - Current title and role
   - Time at company
   - Previous roles
   - Education

2. **Career Path**
   - Industry background
   - Notable companies
   - Promotion patterns
   - Specializations

3. **Recent Activity**
   - Posts or articles shared
   - Comments on others' posts
   - Professional interests
   - Thought leadership topics

4. **Connections**
   - Shared connections?
   - Common background (school, company)
   - Industry network
   - Potential intro paths

## Finding Hidden Insights

### Company Posts Analysis

**Look For**:
- Product launches → Shows priorities
- Hiring announcements → Team growth
- Awards/recognition → Recent wins
- Customer stories → Success metrics
- Employee spotlights → Culture signals
- Event participation → Industry positioning

**Extract**:
- Timing (when posted)
- Engagement (reactions)
- Tone (proud, humble, excited)
- Frequency (active vs. dormant)

### Employee Profiles Mining

**Search Pattern**:
"[Company Name] [Department]" in LinkedIn search

**What to Check**:
- Common backgrounds
- Career progression patterns
- Tenure lengths
- Team sizes
- Technologies used

### Competitive Research

**Quick Scan**:
1. Who do they follow?
2. Who follows them?
3. Similar companies section
4. Industry hashtags used

## LinkedIn Search Tips

### Finding Companies
```
Search: "[Industry] [Stage/Size]"
Example: "SaaS startup Series B"
```

### Finding Hiring Managers
```
Search: "[Role] at [Company]"
Example: "VP Marketing at TechCo"
```

### Finding Similar Roles
```
Search: "[Job Title] [Location]"
Example: "Marketing Manager remote"
```

### Finding Connections
```
Search: "People who work at [Company]"
Filter: 1st or 2nd connections
```

## Red Flags to Watch

### Company Red Flags
- ⚠️ No recent posts (6+ months)
- ⚠️ Very low engagement on posts
- ⚠️ Mass layoff announcements
- ⚠️ Negative glassdoor reviews (cross-reference)
- ⚠️ High employee turnover (check tenure)

### Job Posting Red Flags
- ⚠️ Same role posted repeatedly
- ⚠️ Vague responsibilities
- ⚠️ Unrealistic requirements
- ⚠️ "Urgent" or "immediate" language
- ⚠️ Aggressive or negative tone

### Hiring Manager Red Flags
- ⚠️ Very short tenure (< 6 months)
- ⚠️ History of frequent job changes
- ⚠️ No activity or engagement
- ⚠️ Mismatched role description

## Time-Saving Shortcuts

### Quick Research (5 min)
- Company tagline
- Last 3 posts
- Employee count
- 1 specific recent news

**Good For**: Initial screening, multiple applications

### Standard Research (10 min)
- Company overview
- Last 5-10 posts
- Team structure
- 2-3 specific insights

**Good For**: Serious applications, targeted outreach

### Deep Research (20 min)
- Full company analysis
- Hiring manager profile
- Competitive positioning
- 5+ personalization hooks

**Good For**: Dream roles, referral connections

- Research 2-3 specific companies
- Complete personalization hooks
- Draft outreach emails

- Verify all facts still current
- Update saved research
