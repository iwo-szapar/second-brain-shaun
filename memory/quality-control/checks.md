# Quality Control Checks

## Purpose
Prevent AI hallucinations and ensure high-quality output that sounds human and professional.

**Key Concern**: AI hallucinations and poor quality output
**Solution**: Multi-layer verification process

---

## Pre-Send Checklist

### 1. Fact Verification ‚úì
Every claim must be verifiable:

- [ ] Company name spelled correctly
- [ ] Job title matches posting exactly
- [ ] Company facts come from LinkedIn or website
- [ ] No assumed or guessed information
- [ ] Recent news/updates are actually recent (check dates)
- [ ] Product/service descriptions match company's own language

**Red Flags**:
- ‚ùå "Industry-leading" without evidence
- ‚ùå Specific metrics not from source material
- ‚ùå Claims about company culture without evidence
- ‚ùå Assumed pain points or challenges

### 2. Hallucination Detection ‚úì
Common AI hallucination patterns to watch for:

- [ ] **Made-up statistics**: "95% of customers" when not in source
- [ ] **Invented quotes**: Attributing words to people
- [ ] **False specifics**: Exact dates, numbers, or names not in research
- [ ] **Assumed relationships**: "Your partnership with X" when not confirmed
- [ ] **Generic claims**: "Award-winning" without verification
- [ ] **Future predictions**: "You're planning to X" without source

**Test**: Can you cite the exact source for every claim?

### 3. Human Voice Test ‚úì
Does it sound like a real person wrote it?

- [ ] Read it out loud‚Äîdoes it flow naturally?
- [ ] Would you actually say these words?
- [ ] Is sentence structure varied (not robotic)?
- [ ] Are there personal touches (not just facts)?
- [ ] Does it avoid corporate jargon?
- [ ] Is there genuine enthusiasm (not fake)?

**Red Flags**:
- ‚ùå Every sentence same length
- ‚ùå Overly formal language
- ‚ùå Generic phrases ("I am writing to express...")
- ‚ùå No personality or voice

### 4. Specificity Test ‚úì
Generic = AI. Specific = Human.

- [ ] Could this email work for another company? (Should be NO)
- [ ] Are there at least 2 company-specific details?
- [ ] Is there at least 1 role-specific detail?
- [ ] Do examples relate directly to the opportunity?
- [ ] Is the "why this company" section unique?

**Red Flags**:
- ‚ùå "Your innovative approach" (generic)
- ‚ùå "Industry leader" without specifics
- ‚ùå "Great company culture" without evidence
- ‚ùå Could apply to 100 companies

### 5. Tone Calibration ‚úì
Professional but human balance:

- [ ] Confident without arrogance
- [ ] Enthusiastic without desperation
- [ ] Professional without stiffness
- [ ] Warm without being casual
- [ ] Direct without being pushy

**Scale**: 1 (too casual) to 10 (too formal). Target: 5-7.

## Revision Process

### Step 1: Fact Check (5 min)
- Open source material
- Verify each claim
- Remove anything unverifiable

### Step 2: Specificity Boost (3 min)
- Replace generic phrases with specifics
- Add 1-2 more concrete details
- Tighten language

### Step 3: Voice Check (2 min)
- Read out loud
- Adjust awkward phrasing
- Ensure natural flow

### Step 4: Final Scan (1 min)
- Spell check
- Name check
- Link check (if any)

**Total Review Time**: ~10 minutes per email
