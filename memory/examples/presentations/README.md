## What to Include Here

### Quality Criteria
Add presentation materials that demonstrate:
- **Clear structure**: Logical flow that builds to a conclusion
- **Visual hierarchy**: Information prioritization on each slide
- **Engaging delivery**: Speaker notes or delivery style documented
- **Audience-appropriate**: Right depth for the audience level
- **Strong outcomes**: Well-received, achieved objectives

### Ideal Count
**4-6 varied examples** covering:
- Client pitch decks
- Workshop/training materials
- Internal strategy presentations
- Conference/speaking presentations
- Onboarding decks
- Process/methodology handbooks

## What the AI Will Learn

From your presentation examples, the AI learns:

### Information Architecture
- How you structure arguments slide by slide
- What you show vs. what you say
- How you build narrative momentum
- Where you place key insights for impact

### Visual Communication
- Your design aesthetic and simplicity level
- How you use visuals to support (not replace) ideas
- What formatting patterns you repeat
- How you balance text and whitespace

### Audience Adaptation
- How you adjust complexity by audience
- What you assume they know vs. explain
- How you engage different audience types
- When you use stories vs. frameworks

### Delivery Style
- Your speaking voice and tone
- How you transition between slides
- When you pause for questions/interaction
- How you close and call to action

## Presentation Types

- **Purpose**: Selling services, ideas, or partnerships
- **Include**: Problem → Solution → Approach → Value → Next Steps

### Workshop Materials
- **Purpose**: Educational/training sessions
- **Include**: Learning objectives → Content → Activities → Takeaways
- **Best for**: Teaching AI your teaching methodology

### Conference Talks
- **Purpose**: Thought leadership and authority building
- **Include**: Hook → Insight → Framework → Examples → CTA

- **Include**: Context → Analysis → Recommendations → Implementation
- **Best for**: Teaching AI your consulting delivery style

- **Purpose**: Documenting methodologies and frameworks
- **Include**: Overview → Steps → Best Practices → Examples

## Slide Structure Patterns

Document recurring patterns you use:

### Opening Patterns
- **Hook slide**: Provocative question or stat
- **Context slide**: "Here's where we are"
- **Agenda slide**: "Here's where we're going"
- **Story opener**: Personal or client example

### Middle Patterns
- **Framework slide**: Visual model or diagram
- **Evidence slide**: Data, testimonials, case studies
- **Comparison slide**: Before/after, old way/new way
- **Process slide**: Step-by-step flow

### Closing Patterns
- **Recap slide**: Key takeaways (3-5 bullets)
- **CTA slide**: What happens next
- **Q&A slide**: Open discussion prompt
- **Thank you slide**: Contact info

## Deck Types

### 1. Sales/Pitch Deck
**Typical flow**: 8-12 slides
1. Hook (problem or opportunity)
2. Who we are (brief credibility)
3. The problem (their pain points)
4. Our approach (differentiation)
5. What you get (deliverables/outcomes)
6. How it works (process overview)
7. Results (social proof)
8. Investment (pricing framework)
9. Next steps (clear CTA)

### 2. Workshop Deck
**Typical flow**: 15-25 slides
1. Welcome & objectives
2. Context setting
3. Core concept introduction
4. Framework explanation
5. Examples (2-3 slides)
6. Exercise 1 instructions
7. Debrief/learning
8. Deep dive section
9. Exercise 2 instructions
10. Application discussion
11. Takeaways & resources

### 3. Strategy Presentation
**Typical flow**: 10-15 slides
1. Executive summary
2. Current state analysis
3. Key findings
4. Strategic recommendations
5. Implementation roadmap
6. Success metrics
7. Resource requirements
8. Next steps
